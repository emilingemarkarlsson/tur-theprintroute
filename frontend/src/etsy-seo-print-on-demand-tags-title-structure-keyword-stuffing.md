# Etsy SEO for print-on-demand: tag limits, title structure, and why keyword stuffing stopped working

---

## Blogg



Etsy SEO for print-on-demand worked differently in 2022 than it does now. The sellers still running keyword-stuffed titles from old guides are not losing to bad luck — they are losing to algorithm behavior that changed in 2024 and penalizes exactly what those guides recommended.

The core shift: Etsy's search algorithm now weights behavioral signals (click-through rate, conversion rate, add-to-cart) more heavily relative to keyword match frequency. A title stuffed with keywords performs worse on CTR — buyers do not click unreadable copy — which tanks the behavioral signals that drive ranking.

**Title structure**

The first 40 characters of your Etsy title are the most heavily weighted. Front-load the buyer-intent phrase: what would someone type when they are ready to buy this specific item?

For a print-on-demand mug: "Funny Cat Mug for Cat Lovers — Ceramic 11oz Coffee Mug Gift" outperforms "Cat Mug Cat Gift Funny Mug Coffee Mug Cat Lover Mug Gift" for two reasons. It is readable (improves CTR) and it front-loads the highest-intent phrase.

Keyword stuffing damages the listing in the same step it tries to help it. The algorithm sees keyword match; the buyer sees junk and does not click; the behavioral signal drops; the ranking drops.

**Tags: use phrases, not single words**

Etsy gives you 13 tags, each up to 20 characters. Each tag should be a multi-word buyer-intent phrase, not a single category label.

"Funny cat mug gift" beats "cat" and "mug" as separate tags. The phrase matches a real search query. The single words compete in oversaturated categories.

Strategy: use your 13 tags to cover 13 distinct buyer-intent queries. Do not repeat the same phrase twice — you waste a tag slot. Use variations: "cat mug birthday gift," "cat lover coffee mug," "cat mom gift idea" are three different queries, each specific, each worth a tag.

**Matching title and tags**

Etsy's algorithm confirms relevance when the same phrase appears in both the title and the tags. Pick your three or four highest-intent phrases and make sure each one appears once in the title and once in the tags.

This is the only intentional repetition that helps. Repeating the same phrase across multiple tags does not multiply relevance — it wastes tag slots.

**Long-tail specificity over volume**

Etsy search for print-on-demand is won on specificity. "Personalized nurse mug retirement gift" has lower search volume than "nurse mug" but much higher purchase intent. The buyer who types nine words knows what they want and is more likely to buy.

POD sellers do not need to compete on high-volume generic terms where established sellers with thousands of reviews dominate. They win on specific queries where the intent is precise and the competition is thinner.

**Recency signals**

New listings get a short-term visibility boost from Etsy's recency factor. Listings that stall (no sales, no reviews, no saves) fade from search within weeks.

For POD sellers: if a listing has been live for three months with no sales, do not delete it — iterate it. Change a tag, adjust the title, update an attribute. Small changes can reset the recency signal. Test the new configuration before assuming the product does not sell.

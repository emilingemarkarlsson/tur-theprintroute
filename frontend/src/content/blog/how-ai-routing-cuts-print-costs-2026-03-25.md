## The Hidden Cost of Manual Print Routing

Most print procurement teams still route jobs manually — forwarding PDFs by email, calling local partners to check capacity, and negotiating rates job by job. It works, but it leaves significant money on the table.

A typical enterprise running 500+ print jobs per month loses an average of 18–40% in avoidable costs through suboptimal routing decisions. The culprit isn't laziness — it's the sheer volume of variables involved in each decision.

## What AI Routing Actually Does

AI routing doesn't replace your print partners. It makes better use of them.

When a new job arrives — say, 1,000 A3 posters destined for Stockholm — the system evaluates every available provider against four dimensions:

- **Cost per unit** at the requested quantity
- **ETA** against the required delivery window
- **Capacity** at the time of submission
- **Quality history** for the specific material type

This evaluation happens in milliseconds. The job is assigned to the provider most likely to deliver at the lowest total cost within the required window.

## Real-World Impact

Teams using automated routing consistently report three measurable outcomes:

**Reduced procurement overhead.** Routing decisions that previously took 15–30 minutes of email exchange happen automatically. Operations teams reclaim hours per week for higher-value work.

**Lower average cost per job.** By routing to local providers for short runs and global partners for large volumes, average unit costs drop by 15–40% depending on job mix.

**Fewer missed deadlines.** When a provider's capacity is full, the system routes to the next best option automatically — rather than discovering the problem two days before delivery.

## The Routing Logic

The routing engine combines rule-based logic with machine learning:

1. **Hard rules first** — e.g., all Sweden large-format jobs route to Nordic providers
2. **Soft scoring** — cost, ETA, and quality history scored and weighted
3. **Fallback chains** — if primary provider is unavailable, cascade to alternatives

The result is a deterministic, auditable decision — not a black box. Every routing choice is logged with the reason it was made.

The print industry has undergone significant changes in recent years, driven by advances in technology, shifting consumer behaviors, and the need for increased efficiency. One of the most notable trends in the print industry is consolidation, which has been gaining momentum over the past decade. According to a report by Smithers, the global print market is expected to decline by 1.8% annually from 2020 to 2025, leading to a surge in mergers and acquisitions as companies strive to remain competitive. In this article, we will delve into the trends and implications of print industry consolidation, providing procurement managers, print buyers, and operations leads with practical guidance and actionable insights.

## Understanding Print Industry Consolidation
Consolidation in the print industry refers to the process of companies merging or acquiring other businesses to reduce costs, increase efficiency, and expand their market share. This trend is driven by several factors, including the decline of traditional print products, the rise of digital media, and the need for companies to adapt to changing consumer behaviors. According to a survey by the Printing Industries of America, 64% of printers reported that they had either merged with or acquired another company in the past five years.

### Drivers of Consolidation
The print industry is facing significant challenges, including declining demand for traditional print products, increasing competition, and rising costs. To remain competitive, companies are seeking to consolidate their operations, reduce costs, and improve efficiency. Some of the key drivers of consolidation in the print industry include:
* Decline of traditional print products: The rise of digital media has led to a decline in demand for traditional print products, such as newspapers, magazines, and catalogs.
* Increasing competition: The print industry is highly competitive, with many companies vying for a limited number of contracts.
* Rising costs: The cost of raw materials, labor, and equipment is increasing, making it essential for companies to reduce their costs and improve efficiency.

## Impact of Consolidation on the Print Industry
The consolidation of the print industry has significant implications for procurement managers, print buyers, and operations leads. Some of the key impacts include:
* Reduced competition: Consolidation can lead to reduced competition, which can result in higher prices and reduced innovation.
* Improved efficiency: Consolidation can lead to improved efficiency, as companies eliminate redundant operations and streamline their processes.
* Increased capacity: Consolidation can lead to increased capacity, as companies combine their resources and equipment.

### Benchmarking Consolidation
To understand the extent of consolidation in the print industry, it is essential to benchmark the current market. According to a report by IBISWorld, the print industry has experienced a significant decline in the number of businesses over the past decade, with the number of print shops declining by 23.1% from 2010 to 2020. This decline has been driven by consolidation, as companies seek to reduce costs and improve efficiency.
